The other day I was at a conference, and I was listening to a professor talk about an AI system they built. They were training machine learning models (different models or something) on protein properties like virulence, toxicity, and how easy they are to produce. The idea is that AI is going to make it super easy for us to rapidly develop proteins and try out different things, but we also need to make sure there’s a system in place so the proteins people generate aren’t dangerous.
 
It was a really cool platform. They were using AlphaFold or something to show the 3D models of different proteins, and they had their own things running. They set criteria like, “I want a protein to be this virulent, this toxic, this easy to produce, and potentially this beneficial.”
 
I think that’s one piece of inspiration I had for this Pfizer project.
